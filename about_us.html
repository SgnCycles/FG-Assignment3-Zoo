    Meet The Team

    At Jurassic Parse, we're more than a wildlife park—we're a family of animal lovers on a mission to share the magic of Australia's incredible creatures. Our team is made up of passionate caretakers, biologists, and educators who pour their hearts into every bird, mammal, and reptile in our care. From teaching curious kids about colorful parrots to guiding visitors through the secret lives of playful marsupials and fascinating reptiles, we love bringing animals and people together.

    Every day, we work to create a safe, happy home for our animals while inspiring visitors to appreciate and protect the natural world. We craft fun, hands-on experiences, educational talks, and interactive programs so everyone—no matter their age—can discover, wonder, and learn.

    Curiosity, compassion, and a deep respect for nature guide everything we do. At Jurassic Parse, each visit is a chance to make memories, spark imaginations, and build a lifelong love for wildlife. Come join us, meet our amazing animals, and become part of our growing story of conservation, adventure, and fun!

    Sofia Grant

    Herpetologist

    Sofia Grant is a 34-year-old herpetologist whose fascination with reptiles began when she rescued a wayward grass snake from her family's garden at age nine. She earned her Master's in Wildlife Biology from the University of Edinburgh and has spent the last 11 years specializing in reptile behavior and desert-dwelling species. Sofia joined the zoo six years ago, where she now leads the reptile team in designing innovative, climate-adaptive habitats. Her proudest achievement is spearheading a successful breeding program for a critically endangered gecko species, earning recognition across European conservation networks. Outside work, she's an avid rock climber and macro-photographer, known for capturing stunning close-ups of reptile scales and amphibian eyes. Sofia thrives on challenging fieldwork, enjoys mentoring new keepers, and dreams of co-authoring a global field guide to venomous snakes.
